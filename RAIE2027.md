# Proposed 5th International Workshop on Responsible AI Engineering (RAIE'27)

***Proposal under preparation for [49th IEEE/ACM International Conference on Software Engineering (ICSE 2027)](https://conf.researchr.org/home/icse-2027), Dublin, Ireland, 25 April - 1 May 2027***

**Status:** RAIE'27 is currently being prepared as a workshop proposal for ICSE 2027. Details on acceptance, final workshop date, paper submission deadlines, and program will be updated after ICSE workshop decisions.

ICSE 2027 will be held in Dublin, Ireland, at The Convention Centre Dublin from Sunday, 25 April to Saturday, 1 May 2027. Core conference days will be Wednesday, 28 April to Friday, 30 April 2027. ICSE 2027 workshops are planned for Sunday, 25 April to Tuesday, 27 April, or Saturday, 1 May 2027.

### Theme & Goals

The rapid advancements in AI, particularly foundation models and large language models (LLMs), have attracted significant global interest and raised substantial concerns about responsible AI and AI safety. While individual models are powerful, it is the broader AI systems that integrate models with tools, data, controls, workflows, and human oversight that are ultimately deployed and have real-world impact. These AI systems, especially autonomous agents and multi-agent systems, require careful system-level engineering to ensure responsibility, safety, reliability, and trustworthiness.

In recent years, governments, standards bodies, research organizations, and enterprises have issued many responsible AI and AI safety principles, regulations, and guidelines. However, these are often high-level and do not provide concrete guidance for technologists on how to implement responsible and safe AI systems in practice. New and improved software and AI engineering approaches are required to ensure that AI systems are trustworthy throughout their lifecycle and trusted by those who use and rely on them.

Achieving responsible AI engineering requires a comprehensive understanding of human expectations, deployment context, risk, governance, and the lifecycle of AI-enabled systems. This proposed workshop aims to bring together researchers and practitioners in software engineering, AI, ethics, social sciences, safety, standards, and regulatory domains to build a community focused on responsible and safe AI engineering practices.

The primary objectives of the proposed workshop are to:

1. Share cutting-edge software and AI engineering methods, techniques, tools, and real-world case studies that can help ensure responsible AI and AI safety.
2. Facilitate discussions among researchers and practitioners from diverse fields to address responsible AI and AI safety engineering challenges.
3. Promote new and improved engineering approaches to ensure AI systems are trustworthy and trusted throughout their lifecycle.

### Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

- Requirements engineering for responsible AI and AI safety
- Responsible-AI-by-design and AI-safety-by-design software architecture
- Verification and validation for responsible AI and AI safety
- DevOps, MLOps, LLMOps, and AgentOps for responsible and safe AI systems
- Development processes for responsible and safe AI systems
- Responsible AI and AI safety evaluation tools and techniques
- Reproducibility and traceability of AI systems
- Trust and trustworthiness of AI systems
- Responsible AI and AI safety governance
- Diversity and inclusion in the responsible AI ecosystem, including humans, data, processes, algorithms, systems, and governance
- Operationalisation of laws, regulations, and standards
- Human aspects of responsible AI and AI safety engineering
- Evaluation of AI agent behaviours, consequences, reasoning reliability, and risk
- Human-AI interaction, collaboration reliability, and user-centric evaluation
- Responsible AI and AI safety engineering for foundation-model-based AI systems
- Case studies from high-priority domains such as financial services, science, health, environment, and energy
