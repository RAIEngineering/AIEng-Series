# 5th International Workshop on Responsible AI Engineering (RAIE'27)

***Proposal under preparation for [49th IEEE/ACM International Conference on Software Engineering (ICSE 2027)](https://conf.researchr.org/home/icse-2027), Dublin, Ireland, 25 April - 1 May 2027***

**Status:** RAIE'27 is currently being prepared as a workshop proposal for ICSE 2027. Details on acceptance, final workshop date, paper submission deadlines, and program will be updated after ICSE workshop decisions.

### ICSE 2027 Context

ICSE 2027 will be held in Dublin, Ireland, at The Convention Centre Dublin from Sunday, 25 April to Saturday, 1 May 2027. Core conference days will be Wednesday, 28 April to Friday, 30 April 2027. ICSE 2027 workshops are planned for Sunday, 25 April to Tuesday, 27 April, or Saturday, 1 May 2027.

### Theme & Goals

The rapid advancements in AI, particularly large language models (LLMs) and their applications, have attracted significant global interest and raised substantial concerns about responsible AI and AI safety. While LLMs are impressive examples of AI models, it is the broader compound AI systems that integrate models with other components for function and quality/risk control that are ultimately deployed and have real-world impact. These AI systems, especially autonomous agents and multi-agent systems, require careful system-level engineering to ensure responsible AI and AI safety. Responsible AI and AI safety requirements need to be measurable, verifiable, and monitorable throughout the AI system engineering lifecycle.

In recent years, governments, standards bodies, research organizations, and enterprises have issued many responsible AI and AI safety principles, regulations, and guidelines. However, these are often high-level and do not provide concrete guidance for technologists on how to implement responsible and safe AI systems in practice. Developing responsible AI systems goes beyond fixing traditional software "bugs" or providing theoretical guarantees, particularly as multi-agent systems introduce risks from inter-agent interactions, emergent behaviours, and compounding uncertainties. New and improved software and AI engineering approaches are required to ensure that AI systems are trustworthy and safe throughout their lifecycle.

Diversity and inclusion in AI are crucial for ensuring that technology fairly represents and benefits all segments of society. By incorporating diverse perspectives within data, processes, systems, and governance, AI systems can be more ethical and effective in addressing the needs of diverse and under-represented users, while fostering transparency, accountability, and trustworthiness.

A critical yet underexplored dimension of responsible AI engineering is effective human oversight during deployment and operation. Users must maintain critical judgment when consuming AI-generated outputs, resisting automation bias and over-reliance. At the same time, generative AI systems can produce outputs at a volume far exceeding human review capacity. This demands novel engineering approaches to risk-proportionate human evaluation, strategic allocation of limited human attention, tiered review architectures, and principled decisions about what can be automated versus what requires human judgment.

Achieving responsible AI engineering requires a comprehensive understanding of human expectations, deployment context, risk, governance, and the lifecycle of AI-enabled systems. This proposed workshop aims to bring together researchers and practitioners in software engineering, AI, ethics, social sciences, safety, standards, and regulatory domains to build a community focused on responsible and safe AI engineering practices. The workshop will focus on requirements engineering, architecture and design, verification and validation, operational processes, human oversight mechanisms, and evaluation frameworks for responsible deployment.

The primary objectives of the proposed workshop are to:

1. Share cutting-edge software and AI engineering methods, techniques, tools, and real-world case studies that can help ensure responsible AI and AI safety.
2. Facilitate discussions among researchers and practitioners from diverse fields to address responsible AI and AI safety engineering challenges.
3. Promote new and improved engineering approaches to ensure AI systems are trustworthy and trusted throughout their lifecycle.

### Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

- Requirements engineering for responsible AI and AI safety
- Responsible-AI-by-design and AI-safety-by-design software architecture
- Development processes, operations, reproducibility, and traceability for responsible AI systems, including DevOps, MLOps, LLMOps, and AgentOps
- Verification, validation, and evaluation for responsible AI and AI safety
- Harness engineering for ensuring responsible AI systems
- Evaluation of AI agent behaviours and consequences, including reasoning reliability and risk analysis
- Human aspects of responsible AI and AI safety engineering, including trust, trustworthiness, human oversight, user understanding, and organisational adoption
- Human-AI interaction and collaboration reliability, user-centric evaluation, and scalable human oversight under resource constraints
- Socio-technical evaluation and organisational adoption of responsible AI practices
- Privacy, security, misuse prevention, and abuse-resistance in responsible and safe AI systems
- Fairness, diversity, and inclusion in the responsible AI ecosystem, including humans, data, processes, algorithms, systems, and governance
- Responsible AI and AI safety governance
- Operationalisation of laws, regulations, and standards, including the EU AI Act
- Case studies from high-priority domains such as financial services, scientific discovery, health, environment, and energy

### Workshop Format

RAIE'27 is planned as a one-day, in-person workshop, preferably before the main ICSE 2027 conference, subject to ICSE scheduling. The workshop is expected to be highly interactive, including invited keynotes and paper presentations.

### Target Audience

The target audience includes academic researchers and industry practitioners with experience in the development, governance, and responsible use of AI systems. The workshop aims to attract a diverse mix of participants to foster interdisciplinary collaboration and knowledge exchange.

### Contributions

If accepted, the workshop is expected to invite:

- Research or experience full papers, up to 8 pages
- Short research or experience papers, up to 4 pages
- Extended abstracts, up to 5 pages

Final contribution types, page limits, proceedings arrangements, and submission instructions will be confirmed after ICSE 2027 workshop decisions.

### Submission Guidelines

All submissions must be in English and in PDF format. Papers must not exceed the stated page limits. RAIE'27 is expected to use a single-anonymous review process, with each submission receiving three independent reviews followed by a virtual decision meeting. Detailed submission policies and guidelines will follow ICSE 2027 workshop policies and the applicable ACM/IEEE formatting requirements.
